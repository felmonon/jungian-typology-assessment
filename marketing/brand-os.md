# Brand Diagnosis

## Assumptions

- Market category: Jungian cognitive-functions test, MBTI alternative, self-reflection assessment.
- Target customer: typology-aware searchers who know MBTI language, feel mistyped or over-labeled, and want a clearer function-stack map.
- Painful problem: conflicting personality results create more doubt than insight.
- Emotional desire: "I want a structured answer that feels inspectable, not another label I have to defend."
- Existing alternatives: 16Personalities, Sakinorva, Keys2Cognition, Mistype Investigator, Michael Caloz, IDRlabs, Reddit typing threads, YouTube explainers.
- Unfair advantage: TypeJung gives the free core map first, then sells optional interpretation after the result has earned trust.
- Credibility proof: 42-question assessment, all 8 functions, dominant-inferior axis, sample report, share pages, one-time Stripe checkout, current production traffic and early paid conversion.
- Conversion action: start the free assessment, then upgrade to Insight or Mastery after seeing the map.

## What TypeJung Actually Does

TypeJung helps people inspect the cognitive-function pattern behind their type label. The product is strongest when it stops trying to sound like a prettier MBTI test and owns a sharper promise: show the structure underneath confusing results.

## Who It Is Really For

Not casual quiz takers. Not clinical users. Not people who want an authority to hand them a permanent identity.

TypeJung is for people already searching phrases like "INFJ vs INFP," "dominant function test," "Sakinorva alternative," and "why does my MBTI keep changing?" They are warm, skeptical, and tired of shallow certainty.

## Why Anyone Should Care Now

The category is noisy. Personality tests are easy to take but hard to trust. TypeJung can win by becoming the calm, inspectable alternative for people who have outgrown label-first results.

## What Makes It Different

- It maps all 8 functions instead of hiding the score shape behind a label.
- It frames the dominant-inferior axis as the useful growth tension.
- It makes the free result visible before checkout.
- It sells one-time interpretation, not a subscription.
- It gives users language for stress, relationships, and growth instead of just type identity.

## Shipping Rules

Every public TypeJung surface should pass this three-part test:

1. **Does it name the confusion?** The customer should immediately recognize the moment: changing MBTI results, nearby types blurring together, or stress making them act unlike themselves.
2. **Does it show the mechanism?** The answer should be the visible function-stack map, all 8 functions, and the dominant-inferior tension.
3. **Does it reduce purchase anxiety?** The user should understand that the map is free first, the sample report is visible, and paid depth is optional and one-time.
