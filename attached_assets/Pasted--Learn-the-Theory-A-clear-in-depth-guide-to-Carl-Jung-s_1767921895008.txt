# Learn the Theory

A clear, in-depth guide to Carl Jung's psychological typology—the real foundation behind personality theory.

## Why Jung Created Typology

In 1921, Swiss psychiatrist Carl Gustav Jung published *Psychological Types*, a groundbreaking book. It wasn't about putting people in boxes or labels. Jung wanted to understand **why we all see and judge the world differently**—and how this "one-sidedness" shapes our strengths, blind spots, and personal growth.

> "The classification of individuals means nothing, nothing at all. It is only the instrument... To understand them, you have to be on the spot."  
> — Carl Jung

Jung noticed that everyone leans toward certain ways of thinking, feeling, perceiving, and deciding. This creates natural biases—like wearing tinted glasses. Typology helps us see our "tint" so we can grow beyond it through **individuation** (becoming a more whole, unique self).

**Simple Analogy**: Imagine driving a car. Some people love the accelerator (outward action), others prefer the brakes (inner reflection). Some focus on the map (logic/values), others on the scenery (details/possibilities). Your "driving style" is your type—but the goal is learning to drive well in all conditions.

## How This Assessment Differs from MBTI

The popular Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) uses forced-choice questions (e.g., "Logic OR feelings?") and outputs 16 fixed "types" (like INFP).

This assessment is different:
- It follows the **Singer-Loomis method** (created by Jungian analysts).
- Measures **8 cognitive processes independently** on a scale—no forcing opposites.
- Shows your **nuanced profile** (e.g., you can be high in both logical organization and group harmony).
- Focuses on **self-reflection and growth**, not labels.

No 4-letter codes. No rigid boxes. Just a mirror for your current psychic "configuration."

## The Foundation: Psychic Energy ("Libido")

Jung saw the mind as a dynamic system with energy called **libido** (not just sexual—general life force, like motivation or attention).

This energy flows in patterns, creating habits in how we engage reality.

**Analogy**: Libido is like water in a river. It naturally flows one main way—but dams or blocks cause floods (unconscious eruptions).

## The Two Attitudes: Where Your Energy Flows

Libido flows in one primary direction—this creates **Extraversion** or **Introversion**. (Not about being shy/outgoing—it's deeper orientation.)

### Extraversion → Outward Flow
Energy moves **from you to the world** (object = external reality, people, facts).

- The world "pulls" your attention.
- You adapt by engaging, influencing, exploring.
- **Strengths**: Action-oriented, adaptable, confident in new situations.
- **Blind Spots**: Can overlook inner needs; fear boredom/solitude.
- **Example**: At a party, you dive in—chat, organize games, feed off the energy.

> "The extravert expends energy... propagating their nature into the world." — Jung

### Introversion ← Inward Flow
Energy moves **from the world back to you** (subject = inner impressions, values).

- You filter the world through your inner lens first.
- Caution toward external demands; protect inner space.
- **Strengths**: Deep reflection, independence, concentration.
- **Blind Spots**: Can withdraw too much; inertia or secrecy.
- **Example**: At a party, you observe first, process internally, join selectively.

> "The introvert... tries to withdraw libido from the object, as though to prevent the object from gaining power over him." — Jung

**Key**: Everyone has both—but one dominates consciously. The opposite builds in the unconscious (compensation).

## The Four Functions: How You Process Reality

We take in and evaluate experience via four basic "tools." Two pairs of opposites.

### Perceiving Functions (Irrational – Gather Info, No Judgment)
"Irrational" means "beyond reason"—they just register data.

- **Sensation**: "What exists right now?" Concrete senses (sight, touch, body signals). Includes raw emotions (affect) as internal sensations.
  - Focus: Facts, details, present reality.
  - Example: Noticing textures, flavors, physical energy in a room.

- **Intuition**: "Where is this going?" Unconscious patterns/possibilities.
  - Focus: Hunches, future potentials, connections.
  - Example: Sudden "aha!" insights or seeing hidden meanings.

### Judging Functions (Rational – Evaluate/Decide)
Make ordered judgments.

- **Thinking**: "What is true/logical?" Analyzes connections, principles.
  - Focus: Objective facts, systems, "true/false."
  - Example: Building arguments, solving puzzles logically.

- **Feeling**: "What is valuable?" Assigns worth (good/bad, like/dislike).
  - **Important**: This is **rational valuation**, not raw emotion. Emotion = bodily affect (sensation). Feeling = systematic "yes/no" based on values.
  - Example: Deciding something is "beautiful" or "unfair" because it fits your value system.

**Law of Opposites**: Functions compensate. Strong Thinking → weak/unconscious Feeling (and vice versa). Strong Sensation → weak Intuition.

**Analogy**: Like left/right hand—one dominant, the other underdeveloped but still there (and sometimes "possesses" you under stress).

## The 8 Function-Attitudes: The Building Blocks

Combine attitudes + functions → 8 distinct processes. Each has unique flavor.

(Clickable cards with short descriptions + examples + shadow sides.)

- **Te**: Extraverted Thinking – Efficient external systems.
- **Ti**: Introverted Thinking – Precise inner principles.
- **Fe**: Extraverted Feeling – Group harmony/values.
- **Fi**: Introverted Feeling – Personal authenticity/values.
- **Se**: Extraverted Sensation – Immersive present reality.
- **Si**: Introverted Sensation – Detailed inner impressions/memory.
- **Ne**: Extraverted Intuition – Brainstorming possibilities.
- **Ni**: Introverted Intuition – Deep foresight/symbols.

## The Function Hierarchy: Your Psychic "Stack"

Functions aren't equal—one dominates consciousness.

- **Dominant (Hero)**: Your main tool—strong, identified with ("This is me").
- **Auxiliary (Good Parent)**: Supports dominant (opposite pair: e.g., Thinking dom → Sensing/Intuition aux).
- **Tertiary (Eternal Child)**: Playful but underdeveloped.
- **Inferior (Soul Image)**: Most unconscious—archaic, powerful.

> "The inferior function is the ever-bleeding wound... but through it the unconscious can always come in."  
> — Marie-Louise von Franz

**Note**: Positions like "Hero/Parent" are modern extensions (John Beebe)—helpful metaphors, not Jung's exact words.

## The Grip: When Stress Takes Over

Under pressure, the inferior "erupts" primitively—feels "not like you."

- Signs: Foreign behaviors, hypersensitivity, obsession with ignored things.
- Example: Logical type → sudden irrational emotions; reality-focused type → paranoid hunches.

## Common Misconceptions

- "Feeling = emotional": No—Feeling is rational value judgment. Raw emotion = sensation (affect).
- "Introvert = shy": No—it's energy direction (inner focus).
- "Type is fixed": No—changes with life/growth.
- "Better types exist": No—all have strengths/blind spots.

## Individuation: The Real Goal

Jung didn't want you to "find your type" and stop. Typology reveals **one-sidedness**—your strengths create blind spots.

**Individuation**: Lifelong integration of opposites → more whole Self.

- First half life: Develop dominant/auxiliary (adapt to world).
- Second half: Reclaim tertiary/inferior, confront Shadow → wholeness.

> "Individuation means becoming one's own self... our incomparable uniqueness."  
> — Jung

Your lowest scores? That's your **growth edge**—the "treasure hard to attain."
